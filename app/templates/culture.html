Safety and Culture

Stay safe while exploring this dynamic city.

Stay Safe

Nairobi is a large city with a day time population of close to 4.5 million people. As with many large cities in developing economies,inequality has caused an increase in petty crime. Here are you can steps you can take to stay safe:

  • Avoid walking in the CBD after dark.
  • Avoid using your phone or showing flashy jewellery in the street.
  • Let a friend know if you plan to visit an area of Nairobi you haven't been to before.
  • Keep your car doors locked at all times and don't stop on deserted roads.
  • Book safaris with registered tour operators. Ask to see documentation.
  • Trust your instincts! If you feel uncomfortable in any situation,leave.

If you feel unsure whether a place is safe to go to or not call our local guides and they will be glad to help.

Culture

Kenyans are a diverse community of people with a rich and diverse culture.Here's a heads up on a few things that might take some getting used to.

  • Greetings:Kenyans are a warm and friendly people.Expect a greeting from random strangers.
  • Timing:Outside of a professional setting,time is a fluid concept.Confirm exact timing and follow up if you need precision.
  • Driving:Aggression.
  • PDA:Will be met with bemusement at best.
  • Politics:Often closely linked with ethnicity and some people often get very passionate about it.Avoid these discussions if you can.

In all Nairobi's residents are a friendly people. Keep your wits about you and be respectful of people.You'll enjoy living in this beautiful city.
